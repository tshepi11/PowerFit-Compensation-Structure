How you earn at PowerFit.

This document outlines how compensation works for B2B Sales Partners joining the PowerFit Founding Circle. It is designed to be transparent — you will see exactly what you earn, when it changes, and why.

PowerFit is pre-revenue. There are no signed clients yet. You are joining something being built. These are not co-founder roles. PowerFit has one founder. But the people who join now are founding team members — early, valuable, and rewarded accordingly.

The structure has three phases. You move through them based on your own performance — how many clients you personally bring in. Each partner progresses independently.

This is honest work. Phase 1 is selling a product nobody has heard of. Phase 2 is building momentum. Phase 3 is leading a team and teaching them how to do what you did. None of it is easy. All of it is rewarded.

Why is the commission highest in Phase 1?

Because Phase 1 is the hardest. You are selling a product with zero clients and zero proof. Nobody wants to be first. The company you walk into has never heard of PowerFit. You have no case study, no reference client, no track record to point to. That is why the reward is the highest — you are being compensated for the difficulty and the risk.

The three phases.

Each phase is triggered by your personal client count. You and your partner move through the phases independently.

01
Founding
Clients 1 – 10
No salary. Your income depends entirely on closing deals. This is the hardest phase and the one that pays the most per deal.

What the selling looks like: You will be reaching out to HR decision-makers at companies that have never heard of PowerFit. You will face silence, rejection, and long procurement cycles. Nobody wants to be the first client. You need to be comfortable with that discomfort. The cold email, the follow-up, the meeting that leads nowhere, the one that finally does — that is the work.

10% upfront commission on every deal you close. The annual contract value is R144,000. That means you earn R14,400 every time a client signs, paid when the client pays.

2% monthly recurring for each client you brought in. That is R2,880 per client per month. This is paid to you every month for as long as PowerFit retains that client and you remain in Phase 1 or Phase 2.

At 5 clients, your recurring is R14,400/month. At 10 clients, it is R28,800/month. The recurring builds with every deal you close — it rewards you for staying and for keeping clients happy.

Time expectation: 18 months to reach 10 clients. That is roughly one new client every 7 weeks. If you have not reached 10 clients in 18 months, we will sit down together and assess what is working, what is not, and whether we need to adjust the approach. This is not an automatic termination — it is a performance conversation.
02
Traction
Clients 11 – 20
R20,000 deal bonus — paid in any month you close a deal. This is not a monthly salary. It is compensation for active selling. If you do not close a deal that month, the R20,000 is not paid. Your recurring income continues regardless — that is earned and protected.

7% upfront commission on new deals. That is R10,080 per client. The commission is lower because the risk is lower — you now have proof. You have 10 clients behind you and a product that has been validated.

1% monthly recurring on new Phase 2 clients. That is R1,440 per client per month. Your Phase 1 clients continue paying you 2%. So your recurring income is a mix of Phase 1 rates and Phase 2 rates.

5% equity in PowerFit Wellness Solutions (Pty) Ltd is offered at this stage. It vests over 4 years at 25% per year. This is explained in the Equity tab.

Time expectation: 12 months to reach 20 clients from the start of Phase 2. If progress stalls, we have a conversation.

Why deal-based and not monthly? By Phase 2, your recurring income alone is strong. At 15 clients, you are earning R36,000/month in recurring without signing a single new deal. The R20,000 deal bonus exists to keep the momentum going. PowerFit pays you to sell, not to sit. If you are actively closing, your income is significant. If you stop, your recurring still protects you — but the R20,000 stops until the next deal.
03
Scale
Clients 21+
Salary increases to R60,000/month. Your title becomes Head of B2B Sales. You are no longer selling solo — you are building and managing a sales team. This is a different kind of work. You are recruiting, training, and holding people accountable. You are teaching them how to sell a product you sold when nobody knew what it was. That experience is what makes you the right person to lead this.

5% upfront commission on any deal you personally close. That is R7,200 per client. If you choose to go into the field yourself, you still earn on those deals.

Recurring — the 6-month rule. When Phase 3 starts, recurring on any client signed more than 6 months ago stops immediately. You have already been compensated for those clients. For clients signed within the last 6 months before Phase 3, recurring continues until that client reaches 6 months from signing, then stops. Maximum 6 months into Phase 3, all recurring is done permanently. See the Churn Examples tab for a detailed walkthrough.

Your team's commission is a separate structure that we will agree on together when the time comes. You will also earn an override on deals your team closes — meaning your earning potential does not stop at your own clients. It grows with every person you hire and develop.

All clients belong to PowerFit. You do not own client relationships. You built them, you were rewarded generously for building them, and now they are part of the company you have equity in.

What is expected of you at Phase 3: You are not just managing. You are building a sales function. That means hiring the right people, setting targets, running weekly check-ins, coaching your team through difficult deals, and being accountable for the pipeline. The R60,000 salary reflects this responsibility.

Phase transition discretion.

Phases are triggered by client count. However, the Founder reserves the right to extend Phase 2 terms for up to 6 months. This protects a partner who moves rapidly — for example, from 18 to 22 clients in a short period — and ensures they have had adequate time to benefit from Phase 2 recurring before transitioning. This is exercised in the interest of fairness.

Equity.

At 10 clients, a formal equity conversation takes place. Here is the proposed structure.

Your equity
5%
5% of PowerFit Wellness Solutions (Pty) Ltd. This means you own a piece of the company. If the company is sold, you receive 5% of the sale price. If the company pays dividends, you receive 5%.
Vesting
4 years
Equity vests at 25% per year. After 1 year, you have earned 1.25%. After 2 years, 2.5%. After 4 years, the full 5%. If you leave before 4 years, you keep only what has vested.

What equity is not

  • 1 Equity is not a salary increase. It does not change your monthly income.
  • 2 Equity is not operational control. PowerFit has one founder. The Founder retains majority ownership (70%+) and full decision-making authority.
  • 3 Equity is not guaranteed income. It only has value if the company grows.

Protections

  • ✓ Good leaver / bad leaver. If you are terminated for cause, unvested equity is forfeited.
  • ✓ Right of first refusal. If you want to sell your shares, PowerFit has first option to buy them back. You cannot sell to an outside party without offering PowerFit first.
  • ✓ Non-voting or limited voting. You receive economic rights (dividends, sale proceeds) but not the power to override business decisions.
  • ✓ Shareholders agreement. A formal legal agreement will be drafted when the equity conversation takes place. This protects both you and the company.

Why equity matters.

Equity is the long-term wealth builder. Your commissions and salary are your income today. Equity is your share of what PowerFit becomes tomorrow. If the company reaches 50 clients and someone offers to buy it at 3x revenue, your 5% is worth a significant amount — and you earned it because you were here when it was zero.

The bigger picture.

This is not just a commission structure. It is a journey from founding partner to executive leader.

Phase 1
You are building from zero.
No salary, no proof, no clients. You are selling something that does not exist in the market yet. Every yes you get is a breakthrough. This is the hardest work you will do at PowerFit.
Phase 2
You are building momentum.
The product is proven. You have clients, references, and momentum. A salary kicks in. Equity is on the table. You are no longer surviving — you are building.
Phase 3
You are leading a team.
You are no longer selling solo. You are recruiting, training, and managing a sales team. You are teaching them how to sell a product you sold when nobody knew what it was. Your income comes from salary, equity, and your team's performance. This is not easier. It is different. And it requires you to step up.

Phase 3 is not a pay cut. It is a change in how you earn.

In Phase 1 and 2, you earned by doing the work yourself — every cold email, every meeting, every close. In Phase 3, you earn by building the machine that does the work. You still earn 5% on any deal you personally close. You earn an override on your team's deals. You have equity in the company. And you have a R60,000/month salary that does not depend on whether a prospect picks up the phone.

But let me be clear: Phase 3 is not a reward where you sit back. You are responsible for building a sales function. That means hiring, setting targets, running weekly check-ins, coaching people through deals they are struggling with, and being accountable when the pipeline is not moving. The people you hire will look to you for direction because you are the person who did it first. That is a responsibility, not a title.

The people who go through Phase 1 carry something that cannot be taught. They know the product because they sold it when nobody wanted it. They know the objections because they heard every single one. They know the market because they built the pipeline from scratch. That is what makes them the right person to lead a team.

What you are earning is not just money.

  • 1 Equity in a company you helped build. You are not an employee who joined after the hard work was done. You were here at zero.
  • 2 A leadership title you earned. Head of B2B Sales is not given — it is built through 20 clients, hundreds of conversations, and the credibility that comes from doing the work.
  • 3 A team that reports to you. You built the sales engine. Now you run it.
  • 4 Income from your team's efforts. Every deal your team closes generates revenue for the company and an override for you. Your earning potential does not stop at 20 clients — it grows with every person you hire and train.
  • 5 A founding story. When PowerFit scales, you are not someone who joined after the hard work was done. You are the person who was here when there were zero clients and zero revenue. That means something.

Key rules.

These rules apply across all phases. They are here so there are no surprises.

  • 1 All clients belong to PowerFit Wellness Solutions (Pty) Ltd. You do not own client relationships. You are rewarded for building them through commissions, recurring, and equity — but the client relationship belongs to the company.
  • 2 Recurring is paid for as long as PowerFit retains the client. If a client churns, your recurring from that client stops immediately. This incentivises client retention — your income is tied to keeping clients happy.
  • 3 There is no clawback on upfront commissions. Once paid, the upfront commission is yours regardless of what happens with the client.
  • 4 Recurring stops at Phase 3 — the 6-month rule. When Phase 3 starts, recurring on clients signed more than 6 months ago stops immediately. Recurring on clients signed within the last 6 months continues until that client reaches 6 months from signing, then stops. Maximum 6 months into Phase 3, all recurring is permanently done.
  • 5 If you leave, recurring stops. If you leave PowerFit voluntarily or are terminated, all recurring payments stop immediately from the date of departure. No clawback on upfront commissions already paid. Unvested equity is forfeited. Vested equity is retained subject to the shareholders agreement.
  • 6 Non-compete and non-solicitation. A 12-month non-compete and non-solicitation period applies after departure. You may not contact PowerFit clients or compete directly in the same market for 12 months.
  • 7 Phases are per partner. You and your partner move through the phases independently based on your own client count. If one partner is at Phase 2 and the other is still in Phase 1, each earns according to their own phase.
  • 8 Time expectations. Phase 1: 18 months to reach 10 clients. Phase 2: 12 months to reach 20 clients. These are not automatic terminations. If you are not on track, we sit down and assess together. But there is an expectation to keep moving.
  • 9 The R20,000 in Phase 2 is deal-based. It is paid in any month you close a deal. No deal, no R20,000. Your recurring income is not affected — that is earned and always protected. The R20,000 exists to reward active selling, not to fund comfort.
  • 10 PowerFit has one founder. These are not co-founder roles. PowerFit Wellness Solutions was founded by Tshepiso Power. Founding Circle members are founding team members — early, valuable, and rewarded accordingly — but not co-founders.
  • 11 Team commission is separate. Any sales team you build in Phase 3 will have its own commission structure. That structure will be agreed on between you and the Founder when the time comes. It does not follow the same rates as your personal structure.
  • 12 Coaching. PowerFit has brought on a coach who will work with the entire team including the Founder. Coaching sessions begin from June. This is part of how we grow together — not just as salespeople, but as leaders.

A final note.

This compensation structure was designed to be generous to the people who believe in PowerFit before anyone else does. The recurring commissions, the equity, the phased salary increases — all of it is built to reward the risk you are taking by joining a pre-revenue startup.

In return, PowerFit asks for commitment, consistency, and honesty. If the product is wrong, say so. If the pitch is not landing, tell me. If something is not working, raise it. This is a founding team, not a job. We build this together.
